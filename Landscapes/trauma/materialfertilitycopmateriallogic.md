# **Fertility in Material Logic: Removing Obstacles Rather Than Pursuing Goals**

## **Takeaway**
In Buddhist, Taoist, and “material” logic, fertility is not a goal to be achieved but a **natural state that emerges when obstacles are removed**. The question is not “Does this technique produce fertility?” but “Does this practice remove the conditions that prevent fertility?” This applies both to **biological fertility** and **fertility in love**—the capacity to bond, create, and sustain.

# **1. Material Logic vs. Goal Logic**

## **1.1. Goal Logic**
Western thinking often frames fertility as:

- A target  
- A measurable outcome  
- Something to “achieve” through effort  

This is **teleological**: it assumes a direction and a purpose.

## **1.2. Material Logic**
Buddhist and Taoist traditions use a different logic:

- Fertility is natural  
- Obstacles are artificial  
- Remove the obstacle → the natural state returns  

This is **causal** rather than **teleological**.
