## 1. The shared Laegna framework of infinity

### 1.1 Quantitative infinities as structured layers

In classical analysis, infinity is usually treated as a limit or as a cardinality.  
In Laegna, infinity is **quantitative and layered**:

- Infinity appears as **octaves** of scale (space, time, frequency).
- Each octave is locally finite but globally embedded in a larger infinite structure.
- Transitions between octaves behave like **phase transitions** rather than singularities.

Instead of a single “∞”, Laegna treats infinities as **families of measures**—different ways in which quantities approach, embody, or cross thresholds that behave like infinities.

### 1.2 Infinity as medium, not endpoint

In Laegna, infinity is not the end of a process but the **medium of processes**:

- Motion does not “hit” infinity; it **moves within** an infinity-structured space.
- Physical constants (like the speed of light) are not just upper bounds; they are **octave boundaries**.
- Mathematical divergence is reinterpreted as **coordinate misalignment** between octaves.

This view allows Laegna to reinterpret classical physics and mathematics in terms of **octave-linear** structures: what looks non-linear or divergent in one octave becomes linear and well-behaved when seen across octaves.

### 1.3 Class–instance and infinity

The class–instance model you use in Laegna fits naturally with this infinity framework:

- A **class** is an infinite structure: a topology of possible instances.
- An **instance** is a local collapse of that structure: a finite realization of an infinite pattern.
- Infinity is the **space of possible instances** that a class can generate.

In this sense, every class is already an infinity, and every instance is a **finite cross-section** of that infinity.
