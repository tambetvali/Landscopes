## 1. Introduction: What Scale and Zoom Actually Are

We treat a circle (“CC”) with radius r = 1 as the reference object.

Two independent operations exist:

### SCALE (changes the radius)
- **Scale Z:** r → infinitesimal.  
  The circle collapses inward.  
  Edge loses angles.  
  Topology breaks.  
  The circle becomes an “introvert”: internal coordinate relations vanish.

- **Scale X:** r → 1.  
  The circle stays at its reference size.  
  No topology change.

- **Scale Y:** r → infinity.  
  The circle expands outward.  
  Curvature → 0.  
  Edge becomes flat.  
  Euclidean topology breaks because “zero” must be equidistant from all edge points.

### ZOOM (changes the viewpoint)
- **Zoom Z:** z → infinitesimal.  
  Infinite zoom‑in.

- **Zoom X:** z → 1.  
  No zoom.

- **Zoom Y:** z → infinity.  
  Infinite zoom‑out.

### Cancellation Principle
When SCALE and ZOOM use the same letter (ZZ, XX, YY), they cancel each other:

- Z + Z → infinitesimal scale undone by infinitesimal zoom  
- X + X → normal scale undone by normal zoom  
- Y + Y → infinite scale undone by infinite zoom

The circle’s topology is restored, but the geometry (metric, axes, infinity behavior) changes.

When letters differ (ZX, XY, etc.), topology breaks or transforms.

## 2. Why Z, X, Y Are Meaningful Spaces

Z, X, Y are not arbitrary labels.

- **Z-space:** logarithmic  
- **X-space:** linear  
- **Y-space:** exponential

Projection into the common display space Xp uses:

- Z → log(Z)  
- X → X  
- Y → exp(X)

This ensures that **angles remain 360°** in all three spaces.

### Infinity is relational
Infinity is not a magnitude but a relation:

- Z magnitudes are multiplied by ∞ (dense tesimal fog).  
- Y magnitudes are divided by ∞ (thin tesimal dust).  
- X magnitudes remain finite.

This is why infinite circles become flat, and zero-circles become points.

## 3. Proof: How Scale Breaks Topology

Let the circle be parametrized:

x = r cos θ  
y = r sin θ

### Scale Z: r → 0 (infinitesimal)
- curvature → ∞  
- angles undefined  
- boundary collapses  
- topology breaks

### Scale Y: r → ∞
- curvature → 0  
- boundary becomes flat  
- all tangents → 0°  
- zero must be equidistant from all edge points  
- Euclidean geometry breaks

### Scale X: r = 1
- topology preserved

Thus:

**Scale Z and Scale Y break topology.  
Scale X preserves topology.**

## 4. Proof: How Zoom Restores Topology

Zoom acts on the *representation* of r, not on r itself.

Let r' = scaled radius.  
Let z = zoom factor.

Displayed radius = r' × z.

### If z = r'
Then displayed radius = 1.

Topology returns.

But the coordinate system changes:

- Z → log  
- X → linear  
- Y → exp

Thus:

**Topology is restored, but the number system is replaced.**

This is the core theorem:

> Broken topology by scale breaks the number system.  
> Restored topology by zoom creates a new level where it left off.

## 5. Why Infinite Circles Become Flat

When r → ∞:

- curvature → 0  
- boundary → straight line  
- all tangents → 0°  
- but each boundary point is at a different coordinate  
- zero must be equidistant from all edge points  
- impossible in Euclidean geometry

Thus:

**Infinite circles break Euclidean geometry.  
Zero becomes a direction, not a point.**

## 6. Why Zero-Circles Become Points

When r → 0:

- curvature → ∞  
- angles undefined  
- boundary collapses  
- topology breaks  
- circle becomes a point with no internal directions

Thus:

**Zero-circles break Euclidean geometry by collapsing dimension.**
