A circle (“CC”) behaves differently in different number spaces.  
In Laegna mathematics, the three spaces:

This is not paradoxical — it is the natural consequence of how **zoom** and **scale** interact.

---

## 1. Why Scale Breaks Topology

Topology is the structure of “what is connected to what” and “what angles exist.”

A circle has:

- a boundary  
- curvature  
- angles  
- a center  
- a radius  

When we **scale** a circle by a factor **s**:

- radius → r/s  
- curvature → 1/(r/s)  
- angles → preserved only if r/s > 0  

If **s → ∞**, then:

- radius → 0  
- curvature → ∞  
- angles → undefined  
- boundary → collapses  
- topology → breaks  

This is why **4XZ** (scaled infinitely down, not zoomed) becomes a **single point**.

Topology is broken because the number system cannot represent the internal structure anymore.

---

## 2. Why Zoom Restores Topology

Zoom is the inverse operation:

- zoom factor = z  
- radius after zoom = (r/s) × z  

If **z = s**, then:

- radius → r  
- curvature → 1/r  
- angles → restored  
- boundary → restored  
- topology → restored

> **Zoom restores topology only if the zoom happens in a number space that can represent the restored structure.**

> **Broken topology by scale breaks the number system.

- x = r cos θ  
- y = r sin θ

- x' = (r/s) cos θ  
- y' = (r/s) sin θ  

Topology breaks when r/s → 0.

- x'' = z × x'  
- y'' = z × y'  

If z = s:

- x'' = r cos θ  
- y'' = r sin θ  

The circle returns.

- In **Z‑space**, distances are measured as log(d).  
- In **X‑space**, distances are measured as d.

> **Topology is restored, but the number system is replaced.**
